Faq’s About Gynecologist & Orthopedic

When should I start seeing a gynecologist?

A: Women should start seeing a gynecologist when they turn 18 or when they become sexually active, whichever comes first.

What is a Pap test, and how often should I get one?

A: A Pap test is a screening test for cervical cancer. Women should start getting Pap tests at age 21 and continue getting them every 3 years until age 65, or as recommended by their gynecologist based on their individual risk factors.

What is the best method of birth control for me?

A: The best method of birth control depends on several factors, including a woman’s age, overall health, and sexual activity. Your gynecologist can help you choose the birth control method that is right for you.

What are the signs and symptoms of menopause, and how can I manage them?

A: The signs and symptoms of menopause include hot flashes, night sweats, vaginal dryness, and mood changes. Your gynecologist can recommend various treatments to manage these symptoms, including hormone replacement therapy and lifestyle changes.

 

How often should I get a pelvic exam?

A: Women should get a pelvic exam every 1 to 3 years, depending on their age and individual risk factors.

How can I prevent sexually transmitted infections?

A: Women can reduce their risk of sexually transmitted infections by using condoms consistently and correctly, getting regular screenings, and limiting their number of sexual partners.

What is orthopedics?

A: Orthopedics is the branch of medicine that deals with the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of musculoskeletal disorders and injuries.

What are some common orthopedic conditions?

A: Some common orthopedic conditions include arthritis, fractures, sprains, strains, and tendonitis.

When should I see an orthopedic doctor?

A: You should see an orthopedic doctor if you have persistent pain, swelling, or stiffness in your joints or muscles, or if you have a sports or work-related injury.

What are some non-surgical treatments for orthopedic conditions?

A: Non-surgical treatments for orthopedic conditions may include physical therapy, medication, orthotics or braces, and lifestyle modifications such as exercise and weight management.
