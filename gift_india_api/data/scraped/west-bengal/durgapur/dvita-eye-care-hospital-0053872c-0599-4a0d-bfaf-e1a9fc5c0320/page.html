FREE EYE CAMPS

Pentacam

The Pentacam test is a non-invasive diagnostic tool used to assess the health and structure of the cornea. It uses a rotating camera to capture multiple images of the eye and create a 3D map of the cornea. This information can be used to diagnose and monitor conditions such as keratoconus, corneal dystrophies, and other corneal abnormalities. The Pentacam test is often used by ophthalmologists and optometrists as part of a comprehensive eye exam.
