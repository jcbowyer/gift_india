About 'Enkay Eye Care'

Dr. N.K. Gupta is graduate from All India Institute of Medical Sciences. After his graduation in 1981, he pursued his postgraduate degree in ophthalmology at Dr R.P Centre Institute of Ophthalmology, AIIMS Delhi. He continued to serve at AIIMS and was trained in Strabismus and Retina for three years.

Later he decided to come in practice and started Enkay Eye Centre at Delhi Cantonment in year 1990. Since then he has been in private practice till date and has extended his practice at Janakpuri and Dwarka, New Delhi.

In addition to his clinical experience and dedication towards his patients, he has to his credit various articles published in health columns of Navbharat Times, Hindustan Times, and Times of India.

He has been active member of Indian Medical Association Janakpuri and was awarded for publication of News Bulletin Vision at State level. He has been awarded Best Secretary of the branch at DMA by the then Home Minister Mr. L. K. Advani ji.

He has been an active social worker and served public by his contributions of doing free eye camps and surgeries through his involvement at Lions Club and Rotary Club. He is still active at Rotary Club and has organized seminar and lectures on Eye health at District level.

He is an active member of various organizations
Ex officio and Member Delhi Ophthalmological Society
Member All India Ophthalmological Society
Dr. N.K. Gupta is very passionate about his work and always works towards giving best services to his patients.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

We’ve got clear answers for your peace of mind.

What makes Enkay Eye Care different from other eye clinics?

At Enkay Eye Care, your vision is our priority. We combine advanced diagnostic technology with personalized care to ensure every patient receives the highest quality treatment. From routine eye exams to complex procedures, our expert team is dedicated to delivering results you can see and trust.

How often should I get my eyes checked?

Most people benefit from a comprehensive eye exam every 1–2 years. If you have a vision problem or a health condition like diabetes, more frequent visits may be needed.

What are common signs that I may need glasses?

Blurry vision, eye strain, headaches, and difficulty seeing up close or far away are signs you might need glasses or an updated prescription.

Can you treat dry eyes and eye strain?

Yes. We offer personalized treatments for dry eyes, digital eye strain, and other common discomforts related to prolonged screen time or environmental factors.

Do you offer pediatric eye care?

Absolutely. We provide gentle, thorough eye exams for children to detect and manage vision problems early on.
