FAQ'S

We offer complete paediatric care including vaccinations, cough and fever treatment, growth and nutrition counselling, allergy management, behavioural and developmental assessments, and paediatric surgeries.

It’s recommended to have regular check-ups during infancy, early childhood, and adolescence to monitor growth, development, and overall health. Visit us for personalised advice based on your child’s age and needs.

We provide care for irregular periods, PCOS, menopause, fibroids, infertility, urinary infections, and other women’s health issues — along with routine check-ups and pregnancy care.

You should visit a gynaecologist if you experience irregular periods, heavy bleeding, pelvic pain, fertility concerns, or menopause symptoms. Regular wellness check-ups are also recommended.

Yes, we provide complete vaccination and immunization programs for newborns, infants, and children as per recommended schedules.

Ideally, your first prenatal check-up should be scheduled as soon as you confirm your pregnancy — typically between 6 to 8 weeks of gestation. Early care helps ensure a healthy pregnancy right from the start.

Common early pregnancy symptoms include missed periods, nausea, fatigue, breast tenderness, frequent urination, and mood changes. However, symptoms can vary from person to person.

Yes, moderate exercise like walking, prenatal yoga, or swimming is usually safe and beneficial during pregnancy. Always consult your doctor before starting or continuing any fitness routine.

Avoid raw or undercooked meat, fish high in mercury (like shark or swordfish), unpasteurized dairy, and caffeine in large amounts. A balanced, nutritious diet is key for your baby’s development.

Adolescent consultation focuses on the physical, emotional, and hormonal changes teens experience. It helps address concerns like puberty, menstrual issues, acne, mental health, and reproductive health — ensuring a healthy transition into adulthood.

Adolescents and teenagers should visit a pediatrician for any health concerns. Pediatric care covers all stages from infancy to adolescence, and our pediatricians are well-equipped to handle adolescent issues with the right expertise and sensitivity.

Teenagers should see a doctor if they experience irregular periods, rapid weight changes, mood swings, delayed puberty, or concerns about body image or mental health. Regular check-ups also help with early detection and health education.
