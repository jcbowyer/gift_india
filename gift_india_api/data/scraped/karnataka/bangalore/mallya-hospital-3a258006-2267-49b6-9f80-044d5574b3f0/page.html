    Medical Oncology

    Medical Oncology is dedicated to the diagnosis, treatment, and management of cancer through non-surgical methods, such as chemotherapy, immunotherapy, and targeted therapies. Our team of expert oncologists works closely with other specialists to develop personalized treatment plans tailored to each patient’s unique needs.

    By utilizing the latest advancements in cancer care, we aim to improve outcomes while minimizing side effects. From the initial consultation to ongoing follow-up, we are committed to providing comprehensive, compassionate care throughout every patient's cancer journey.

    Our Services

    Our Medical Oncology department offers a range of services, including:

    • Day Care Chemotherapy for solid malignancies such as cancers of the breast, lung, colon, stomach, prostate, cervix, head and neck, and more.
    • Day Care Chemotherapy for hematological malignancies like Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma, Hodgkin's Lymphoma, Multiple Myeloma, and others.
    • Inpatient Facilities for the treatment of hematologic malignancies, including:
      • Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)
      • Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML)
      • Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia (CLL)
      • Chronic Myeloid Leukemia (CML)

    • Intrathecal Chemotherapy Administration for hematological cancers.
    • Hormonal Treatments for cancers like breast and prostate cancers.

    • Severe or recurrent headaches

    • Weakness/numbness in arms or legs

    FAQ

    1. What symptoms require seeing a neurologist?
    You should see a neurologist if you experience persistent headaches, seizures, dizziness, numbness, weakness, memory loss, tremors, or difficulty with speech, vision, or balance. These symptoms may indicate underlying brain, nerve, or spinal disorders that need timely evaluation.

    2. What does a neurologist treat?
    A neurologist diagnoses and treats conditions affecting the brain, spine, and nerves, including stroke, epilepsy, migraines, neuropathy, Parkinson’s disease, movement disorders, multiple sclerosis, and nerve compression. They provide both medical management and long-term neurological care.

    3. When should I see a neurologist for headaches or migraines?
    Consult a neurologist if headaches are frequent, severe, sudden, or associated with symptoms like nausea, vision changes, numbness, or speech difficulty. Migraines that interfere with daily activities or do not improve with routine medication also require specialist care.

    4. What tests does a neurologist perform for diagnosis?
    Neurologists use EEG, NCV, EMG, MRI, CT scans, and evoked potentials to evaluate brain and nerve function. These tests help identify seizures, stroke, nerve damage, neuropathy, spinal issues, and other neurological disorders accurately.

    5. What is the difference between a neurologist and a neurosurgeon?
    A neurologist specializes in diagnosing and medically treating brain, nerve, and spine disorders, while a neurosurgeon performs surgical procedures for conditions like tumors, severe spine compression, and certain types of epilepsy or stroke. Many patients require the expertise of both specialists.

    6. What are the early signs of a stroke?
    Early stroke symptoms include facial drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech, sudden vision problems, severe headache, or loss of balance. Immediate medical attention is critical — early treatment can save brain function and reduce long-term disability.

    7. How is epilepsy diagnosed and treated?
    Epilepsy is diagnosed using EEG, imaging tests, and clinical evaluation of seizure patterns. Treatment includes anti-seizure medications, lifestyle modification, and for selected cases, advanced options like video EEG monitoring or surgical evaluation.

    8. What causes numbness, tingling, or weakness in the limbs?
    These symptoms may result from nerve compression, neuropathy, vitamin deficiencies, spinal issues, or neurological disorders. A neurologist performs detailed tests to determine the cause and provide targeted treatment to restore nerve function.

    9. Can neurological disorders be cured or managed?
    Some neurological conditions can be fully treated, while others require long-term management to control symptoms and improve quality of life. Early diagnosis combined with personalised treatment helps slow disease progression and reduce complications.

    10. Which hospital is best for neurology in Bangalore?
    Several hospitals offer neurology care in Bangalore, and VSH Hospital is recognized for its experienced neurologists, advanced diagnostics, 24/7 stroke services, and comprehensive treatment for brain and nerve disorders. Patients choose VSH for personalised care, accurate diagnosis, and strong clinical outcomes.
