Ear Disease

  • Ear diseases encompass a range of conditions affecting the ear, such as otitis media (middle ear infection), otitis externa (outer ear infection), and otosclerosis (abnormal bone growth in the middle ear). Symptoms may include pain, hearing loss, ringing in the ears, and discharge. Treatment varies depending on the specific condition and severity.

Nose Disease

  • Nose diseases comprise various ailments affecting the nasal cavity and sinuses, including rhinitis (inflammation of the nasal mucosa), sinusitis (inflammation of the sinuses), nasal polyps (noncancerous growths), and deviated septum (misalignment of the nasal septum). Symptoms often include congestion, nasal discharge, facial pain, and impaired smell.

OUR SPECIALTIES
Tympanoplasty
Tympanoplasty is the surgical procedure performed to repair a perforated tympanic membrane, with/without reconstruction of the ossicles.
read more
Mastoidectomy
Mastoidectomy is a surgical procedure of the temporal bone that opens postauricular air cells by removing the thin bony partitions between them.
read more
Ossiculoplasty
Ossiculoplasty is the surgical restoration of the sound transmitting mechanism of the middle ear. A myriad of prosthetics and surgical techniques.
read more
Stapedectomy
A stapedectomy is surgery to remove a small bone, called the stapes, from the middle ear. The middle ear contains three bones: stapes, incus, and malleus.
read more
Endoscopic Septoplasty
The primary advantage of the endoscopic septoplasty is the ability to reduce the morbidity by limiting the dissection to the area of deviation.
read more
Septorhinoplasty
A septorhinoplasty is an operation to improve the appearance of your nose (rhinoplasty) and to improve how you breathe through your nose.
read more
Adenoid Surgery
Adenoid removal is surgery to take out the adenoid glands. The adenoid glands sit behind your nose above the roof of your mouth in the nasopharynx.
read more
Tonsillectomy
Tonsillectomy is a surgery to remove the tonsils. The tonsils are glands at the back of your throat. The tonsils are often removed along with the adenoid glands.

OTORHINOLARYNGOLOGY

The perfect blend of medical and surgical management.

As the field of medical science has progressed, we have started to diagnose an array of conditions that were previously indiscernible. With that we are also now progressing beyond treating just Recurrent sore throats and Allergic rhinitis to being able to treat Congenital hearing loss and Anterior Skull Base lesions.
ENT is one field where we are able to give medical management for a lot of conditions like Tonsillitis and Adenoid Hypertrphy with Serous otitis media, Seasonal Allergic rhinitis, Otitis media, Oral Ulcers and Laryngitis amongst many others.
While at the same time striking immediately with a surgical precision for Polyps in the Nose, Cholesteatoma in the ear, Tumors of the Neck, Septal Deviation and Vocal Cord lesions.
Latest advances such as Coblation, Microdebrider, CO2 Laser have made our surgeries much more precise and less time consuming. Better camera systems have made visualising the surgical field a breeze and resulted in far better outcomes for patients than in the past.
Here's hoping that the advances reach such a stage where we can prevent illnesses rather than cure them.
