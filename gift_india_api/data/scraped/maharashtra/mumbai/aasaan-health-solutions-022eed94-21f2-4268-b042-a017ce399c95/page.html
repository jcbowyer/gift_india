High blood glucose, weight that won’t move, cravings, PCOS, fatty liver - often one root.

That root is often insulin resistance - the body stops responding to insulin, so it makes more and more of it. And high insulin doesn’t stop at blood glucose.

Picture a rush-hour train so full the platform staff are shoving people in - that’s insulin forcing glucose into cells that are already packed.

  • Blood glucose that keeps climbing even when you’re doing everything right
  • Cravings that feel like a willpower problem but aren’t
  • Weight that won’t move, especially around the middle
  • PCOS symptoms put down to “just the ovaries”
  • Fatty liver picked up on a scan you weren’t expecting
  • And the weight and energy changes that often tangle up with thyroid and menopause

Medication brings the number down - that is what it is designed to do. It does not change why the number is high. The first consultation starts with the why: no generic chart, lifestyle and medication treated as one picture, before anything new is added.
