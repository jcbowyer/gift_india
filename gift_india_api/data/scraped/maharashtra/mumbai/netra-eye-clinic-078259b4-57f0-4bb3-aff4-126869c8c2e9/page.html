Meet the Founder and Director

Dr. Saikumar Gandapodi is the Founder and Director of Netra Eye Institute, where he leads a holistic, integrative approach to vision care. He holds a Master’s degree in Traditional Oriental Medicine from Pacific College of Health and Science and a doctoral degree from Five Branches University, reflecting his extensive academic training in evidence-informed traditional medicine.

In addition to his formal education, Dr. Gandapodi received advanced clinical training in India, where he worked in eye hospitals and gained hands-on experience in ocular diagnostics and modern ophthalmic imaging technologies. This exposure to contemporary ophthalmic diagnostics, combined with his training in traditional medical sciences, shaped his integrative perspective on eye health.

Drawing on this multidisciplinary background, Dr. Gandapodi developed Netra Restoration Therapy (NRT) — a physiology-based eye care program designed to support retinal and optic nerve health by improving ocular blood flow, enhancing neuroretinal resilience, regulating inflammation, and reducing oxidative stress.

Through NRT, Dr. Gandapodi is committed to providing patients with a science-guided, holistic pathway aimed at supporting long-term retinal and optic nerve health.

Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ section answers common questions about care at Netra Eye Institute. It explains our Integrative Eye Care approach, including Netra Restoration Therapy (NRT), what to expect during evaluations, and how our treatments support long-term eye health—helping you make informed decisions about your vision.

1. Where is Netra Eye Institute located, and are there any branch offices available?

Netra Eye Institute is based in South Plainfield, New Jersey, and currently does not have any branch locations.

2. What is Netra Restoration Therapy (NRT)?

Netra Restoration Therapy (NRT) is a holistic eye-care approach based on TCM and Ayurvedic Ophthalmology that integrates acupuncture, herbal medicine, and supportive lifestyle guidance. NRT focuses on improving ocular circulation, regulating inflammation, and supporting the health of the retina, optic nerve, and visual system as a whole. It is designed to complement conventional eye care by addressing functional imbalances that may contribute to chronic or degenerative eye conditions.

3. What is the success rate of Netra Restoration Therapy?

Netra Restoration Therapy has a very high success rate. For most conditions, patients begin to notice marked improvement in their vision within 7 days of treatment.

4. Do you accept my health insurance for the eye treatments?

Netra Restoration Therapy (NRT) is a holistic approach rooted in TCM  and Ayurvedic Ophthalmology, incorporating acupuncture and herbal supplements. Holistic therapies, including acupuncture and herbal medicine for eye conditions, are not covered by health insurance.

5. Why Netra Restoration Therapy is not covered by my insurance?

Netra Restoration Therapy (NRT) is a holistic approach rooted in TCM  and Ayurvedic Ophthalmology, incorporating acupuncture and herbal supplements. Holistic therapies, including acupuncture and herbal medicine for eye conditions, are not covered by health insurance.

6. Why is my eye doctor not aware of Netra Eye Institute and Netra Restoration Therapy?

Your eye doctor may not be aware of Netra and its holistic eye treatments but should be aware on why Vascular Dysregulation, Neurotrophin Deprivation, Neuroprotection, Cytokine Dysregulation, Ocular Inflammation, Oxidative Stress and Ferroptosis are the root cause of vision loss and multiple recent research studies have shown these factors leads to most eye disorders. If they are not aware of these factors you may want to bridge the gap, by providing your doctor with the research articles given under our References section.

7. How long are the treatments?

The duration of treatment might vary from 2 to 4 weeks depending on the eye condition and its severity. Degenerative eye conditions usually require followup treatments.

8. What eye conditons do you treat?

Please see our conditions treated section to see the complete list of eye disorders we treat.

9. Do you offer treatment for children with vision problems?

Yes. Most sought condition in children is myopia (nearsightedness) and Keratoconus. Netra Restoration Therapy helps stop progression and impove vision.

10. Do I have to come in in-person for treatments?

Yes. Patients have to come in to our office for treatments.

11. Can I use my HSA, FSA or HRA for payments?

Yes. Acupuncture is an eligible expense with a flexible spending account (FSA), health savings account (HSA) or health reimbursement arrangement (HRA).
