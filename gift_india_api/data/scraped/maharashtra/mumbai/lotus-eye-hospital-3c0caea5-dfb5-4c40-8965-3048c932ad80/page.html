About Us

Lotus Eye Hospital, is a trust hospital located in Juhu, Mumbai. It is a highly trusted charitable organization renowned for pioneering affordable eye care. Founded in 1971 by the esteemed economist, Late Shri Manu Subedar, who suffered from impaired vision himself, it remains the oldest and sole branch of Lotus Eye Hospital. The institution has consistently delivered quality eye care services, including cataract treatments, to patients not only from across India, but also around the globe. 

Accredited with ISO certification, this non-profit hospital is dedicated to eradicating visual impairments and making quality eye care accessible to all societal strata.

The hospital boasts a team of highly qualified ophthalmologists, optometrists, pediatric ophthalmologists, and trained medical and paramedical staff. Equipped with state-of-the-art medical equipment from reputed brands, Lotus Eye Hospital performs a variety of eye care treatments and procedures, maintaining high ethical standards. Annually, it treats over 100,000 patients in its outpatient department and conducts approximately 5,000 surgeries.

Specialty clinics at the hospital include Cataract, Glaucoma, Cornea, Pediatric Ophthalmology, Retina, and Oculoplasty. 

The hospital is committed to superior patient care and blindness prevention through regular community outreach programs, serving underprivileged communities in Maharashtra's remote areas.

Lotus Eye Hospital collaborates with Orbis International in its pediatric ward to screen and treat children with eye disorders in Mumbai’s slums. The hospital also houses the Lotus College of Optometry, established in 2000 by Late Shri Naval P. Baliwalla. This institution, offering Mumbai’s first four-year degree program in optometry, focuses on developing human resources for eye care services and is actively involved in research, community eye health, and specialized optometry services. At Lotus Eye Hospital, we are dedicated to protecting vision and making a significant difference in the lives of millions of visually impaired individuals.
