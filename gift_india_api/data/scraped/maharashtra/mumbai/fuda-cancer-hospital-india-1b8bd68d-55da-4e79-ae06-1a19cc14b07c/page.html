Cancer Treatment – An Overview

Cancer Treatment encompasses various medical therapies aimed at curing, managing, or alleviating the symptoms of cancer. The approach depends heavily on the specific type, stage, and location of the cancer, as well as the patient's overall health and personal preferences. In this process, specialized oncology teams work together to create a personalized plan, utilizing advanced medicine and technology to target abnormal cell growth.

Treatment is recommended to achieve remission, prevent the cancer from spreading to other parts of the body, or relieve symptoms to improve quality of life. It often involves a combination of therapies such as surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, immunotherapy, and targeted drug therapy.

When Cancer Treatment May Be Recommended

Oncologists will develop and suggest a treatment plan in the following situations:

  • Presence of localized or malignant tumors
  • Recurrent cancer that has returned after initial treatment
  • Metastatic cancer that has spread to other parts of the body
  • To shrink a tumor before primary surgery (neoadjuvant therapy)
  • To destroy remaining cancer cells after surgery (adjuvant therapy)
  • Management of symptoms and pain (palliative care)
  • Preventive measures for individuals with high-risk genetic mutations

Steps Involved in Cancer Care

The oncology journey generally includes the following stages:

Step 1: Comprehensive Diagnosis & Staging

  • Physical examination and medical history review
  • Imaging tests (MRI, CT, PET scans, X-rays)
  • Laboratory tests (Blood tests, tumor marker checks)
  • Biopsy to confirm the presence of cancer cells
  • Genomic testing of the tumor

Step 2: Primary Intervention (Surgery / Local Therapy)

  • Surgical removal of the tumor and surrounding margins.
  • Lymph node biopsies if necessary.

Step 3: Systemic or Radiation Therapies

  • Radiation: High doses of radiation to kill cancer cells.
  • Chemotherapy: Drugs used to destroy rapidly dividing cells.
  • Immunotherapy: Boosting the body's immune system to fight cancer.

Step 4: Monitoring and Follow-Up

  • Regular scans and tests to monitor treatment effectiveness.
  • Managing side effects and adjusting dosages.

Post-Treatment Care

After completing primary cancer treatment, patients are generally advised to:

  • Follow a highly nutritious, balanced diet
  • Avoid smoking, alcohol, and processed foods
  • Attend all scheduled follow-up appointments and scans
  • Take prescribed medications regularly
  • Rest adequately and avoid severe physical strain
  • Manage physical and emotional stress through rehabilitation

Factors Affecting Treatment Success

Success rates and prognosis depend on several vital factors, including:

  • The specific type and grade of the cancer
  • The stage of the cancer at the time of diagnosis
  • The patient's age and overall physical health
  • How the cancer responds to chosen therapies
  • Genetic mutations present in the cancer cells

Emotional and Psychological Support

Navigating cancer can be emotionally challenging. Counseling, psycho-oncology support, and support groups can help individuals and families manage stress, cope with uncertainty, and maintain mental resilience during treatment. Emotional well-being plays an integral role in the healing process.

Advanced Cancer Treatment Options for Patients in Mumbai

Modern, minimally invasive cancer treatments designed to control cancer effectively while ensuring greater comfort and better quality of life.

Modern & Minimally Invasive Cancer Treatment Techniques
  • NanoKnife (Irreversible Electroporation): A precise technology that destroys cancer cells using controlled electrical energy while protecting nearby vital structures.
  • Cryo-ablation Therapy: Uses extreme cold to freeze and eliminate tumour cells with minimal damage to healthy tissues.
  • Targeted Vascular Interventional Therapy: Controls tumour growth by reducing or blocking its blood supply.
  • Percutaneous Chemo-Ablation: Directly delivers chemotherapy into the tumour for focused and effective treatment.
  • Integrated Immunotherapy: Helps boost the body’s immune power to fight cancer cells naturally.
  • Brachytherapy: Internal radiation therapy placed close to the tumour for precise radiation delivery.
  • Photodynamic Therapy: Uses light-activated medicines to selectively attack and destroy cancer cells.
  • “3C+P” Comprehensive Treatment Model: A personalised combination of cryotherapy, chemotherapy, immunotherapy and precision care planning.
  • HepaSphere Therapy: Drug-loaded microspheres mainly used in liver cancer to block the tumour’s blood supply.
  • Microwave Tumour Ablation: Uses microwave energy to heat and safely destroy tumour tissues.
  • Microwave Hyperthermia: Localised heating that weakens cancer cells and enhances treatment effectiveness.
  • Supportive Ozone Therapy: Helps improve oxygen circulation and supports overall wellness during treatment.

Why These Treatments Are Valuable for Patients in Mumbai

These advanced treatment options are especially beneficial for patients in Mumbai who need non-surgical or minimally invasive cancer solutions, particularly when major surgery is not suitable or not preferred.

The focus of these therapies is to accurately target cancer cells while protecting healthy tissues. This approach helps in achieving less pain, quicker recovery, fewer side-effects and improved daily comfort. Based on the patient’s condition, multiple treatments can also be combined for personalised care.

These modern techniques are extremely helpful for patients with advanced cancers, recurrent cancer, elderly patients, and those seeking effective cancer control without major surgery. Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about cancer care services available through the Fuda Cancer Centre in Mumbai

Is Fuda Cancer Hospital available in Mumbai?

Yes. Fuda Cancer Hospital is represented in Mumbai through the Fuda Cancer Centre, which operates in partnership with Rekosh Health Care. The centre supports patients with consultation, follow-up treatment care, and palliative care services.

What is the Fuda Cancer Centre in Mumbai?

The Fuda Cancer Centre in Mumbai is a patient support and care centre operating with Rekosh Health Care. It assists patients with medical consultation, follow-up treatment guidance, palliative care, and ongoing cancer support.

Who is the cancer specialist associated with the Mumbai centre?

Dr. Anup Aboti is a senior cancer specialist associated with the Mumbai centre. He is recognised as one of the leading cancer doctors in Mumbai and provides expert consultation, personalised care planning, and long-term patient support.

What services are available at the Mumbai centre?

Services available include follow-up treatment care, palliative care, symptom management, medical consultation, treatment monitoring, and supportive care for cancer patients and their families.

Is follow-up treatment available?

Yes. The centre provides follow-up treatment support, helping patients monitor progress, manage side effects, and receive continued medical guidance after primary treatment.

Is palliative care available?

Yes. Palliative care services are available and focus on pain management, symptom relief, emotional support, and improving quality of life for patients and caregivers.

Which types of cancer are supported?

The Mumbai centre supports patients with breast cancer, lung cancer, liver cancer, gastrointestinal cancers, head and neck cancers, prostate cancer, gynecological cancers, and other complex cancer conditions.

How can patients consult the Mumbai centre?

Patients can visit the centre for in-person consultation or connect for medical guidance, follow-up care, and palliative support through Rekosh Health Care.
